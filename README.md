# Pizza Sales Analytics Dashboard

## 📌 Overview
A two-page Power BI dashboard analyzing a full year (2015) of pizza sales data — covering revenue performance, best/worst sellers, sales trends by day and month, and category/size breakdowns.

## 🎯 Problem Statement
Identify top and bottom performing products, peak ordering days/months, and category-level sales drivers to support menu and inventory decisions.

## 🗂️ Data Source
Self-sourced practice dataset (pizza sales transactions, Jan 2015 – Dec 2015)

## 🛠️ Tools Used
Power BI, DAX, Power Query

## 🔍 Key Analysis
**Page 1 — Best/Worst Performance**
- Top 5 and bottom 5 pizzas by revenue, quantity sold, and total orders
- Identified single best and worst performer across all three metrics

**Page 2 — Sales Trends & Category Performance**
- Daily trend for total orders (by day of week)
- Monthly trend for total orders across the year
- % of sales by pizza category and by pizza size
- Total pizzas sold broken down by category

## 💡 Key Insights
- Generated $817.86K in total revenue across 21,350 orders and 49,574 pizzas sold, averaging 2.32 pizzas per order
- The Thai Chicken Pizza was the top revenue generator, while the Classic Deluxe Pizza led in both quantity sold and total orders
- The Brie Carre Pizza was the weakest performer across all three metrics (revenue, quantity, and orders)
- Orders peaked on Thursdays and Fridays, with July and May as the strongest months
- Classic category pizzas drove the most sales and total orders (26.91% of sales), while Large-size pizzas accounted for 45.89% of all sales by size
